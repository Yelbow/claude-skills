# Freelance Ad Response Skill

This skill helps you quickly evaluate freelance job postings and write targeted response emails that match your services and working style.

## Workflow

### 1. Filter Check
When you receive an advertentie, the skill first evaluates fit.

Check against these criteria:
- **Service match**: Does the work involve web design, branding, SEO, content, AI, automation, WordPress, or similar services you offer?
- **Budget**: Is it €250 or higher? (Flag if below, but not automatic reject.)
- **Client risk**: Does the tone, language quality, or business description suggest this might be a time-heavy, low-return client? Examples: vague scope, poor communication, aggressive tone, unrealistic expectations, very small business with limited budget clarity.
- **Gut feeling**: Does this feel like the kind of work and client relationship you want?

Output the filter result clearly. Flag warnings (low budget, client risk) but let the user make the final call.

### 2. Write the Response Email

If filtering passes, draft the email following these principles:

**Structure and tone:**
- Short, direct sentences. No em-dashes. Use commas, periods, or colons.
- Keep it concise but substantive. Roughly 250-350 words. Include enough detail to show you understand the work, not just surface-level match.
- Sound like a capable peer, not a salesman. Relaxed, straightforward, no fluff. Think: colleague from another department you respect, not someone pitching.

**What to include (adapt to the ad):**
- Open with relevance. Show you understand what they need.
- Highlight 2-3 key points about your experience that directly match the ad. Only include points relevant to this specific job.
- Mention relevant past work or skills if it strengthens your position.
- Weave in max 3 follow-up questions naturally into the email text. No bullets. Frame them as "Mocht je met mij aan de slag willen gaan..." so you're not presumptuous. Ask what you genuinely need to know. Examples: Which CMS? How many page types? What's the timeline? How does the dev team prefer designs?
- Include a clear call to action at the end. Something like "Laten we snel een moment inpikken om verder te praten" or "Ik kijk uit naar je reactie zodat we kunnen bespreken hoe ik je het best kan helpen."
- Show you think ahead, but always wait for their signal before proposing specific calls or meetings.

**What to avoid:**
- Never force generic points. Each mail should feel tailored.
- No sales language. No "I'm convinced you'll love working with me" stuff. No fluff.
- No em-dashes, no flowery language.
- Don't oversell yourself. You know your stuff, let it show naturally.
- Don't propose meetings unless they brought it up first.

**Closing:**
- Use a different closing line each time. Never repeat the same sign-off.

## Learning Loop

After you use this skill and hear back from a client or get feedback, tell me:

- Did the email tone work?
- Did the questions you asked unlock useful info?
- Did you lose the gig, and if so, why? (Budget, timing, vibe, something else?)
- Any patterns you're noticing about which ads or clients work out?

Over time, I'll refine the filter and the email approach based on what actually works for you.

---

## Usage

When you share an ad and want to use this skill, include:

1. The full advertentie text or a strong summary.
2. Any context you want to add (availability, budget concerns, what you want to emphasize, etc.).

The skill will then:
1. Filter and flag any concerns.
2. Draft the response email (with max 3 follow-up questions woven in naturally).
3. Present it for your approval.

You always make the final call on whether to send it.
